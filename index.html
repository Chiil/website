Surface-atmosphere-cloud interactions
and atmospheric dynamics

Our group at Wageningen University studies the fast interactions between the land, sea, and ice surface, the atmospheric boundary layer and clouds, and their role in the large-scale atmospheric circulation.

Three-dimensional simulation

With very high resolution three-dimensional simulations with our own tool MicroHH we aim to increase understanding of the complex interactions between the surface, the atmospheric boundary layer and clouds. Very high resolution allows for studies to the finest details of turbulent flows, but also for large-domain studies of meso-scale and synoptics systems.

Interpretation of observations

Approaching the coupled surface-atmosphere-cloud system from an integrated perspective helps in interpreting observational data. With the help of statistics and the bulk model CLASS, which we maintain in our department, we work on the interpretation of (near) the surface energy and water balance.

Climate modeling

With the help of one-dimensional and three-dimensional climate models, we do research on the link between surface-atmosphere-cloud interactions and the climate system. Special focus lies here on understanding which role small-scale and fast interactions (on the time scale of minutes to hours) propagate to the large spatial and temporal scales of the large-scale circulation.
