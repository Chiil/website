Research

Three-dimensional simulation

With very high resolution three-dimensional simulations with our own tool MicroHH we aim to increase understanding of the complex interactions between the surface, the atmospheric boundary layer and clouds. We study the finest details of turbulent flows, but also do large-domain studies of meso-scale and synoptics systems.

Interpretation of observations

Approaching the coupled surface-atmosphere-cloud system from an integrated perspective helps in interpreting observational data. With the help of statistics, the bulk model CLASS, which we maintain in our department, and one-dimensional climate models, we work on the interpretation of the surface energy and water balance.

Read more »

Climate modeling

With the help of one-dimensional and three-dimensional climate models, we study the link between surface-atmosphere-cloud interactions and the climate system. We focus on understanding how small-scale and fast interactions (on the time scale of minutes to hours) propagate to the large spatial and temporal scales of the large-scale circulation.
